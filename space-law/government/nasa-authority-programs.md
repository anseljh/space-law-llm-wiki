# NASA Organic Authority & Programs

*NASA's organic statute and administrative/program authorities (51 U.S.C. Subtitles II-IV and VI) and NASA's space-flight regulations at 14 CFR Part 1214.*

## Summary

NASA's authority derives from the National Aeronautics and Space Act, codified at 51 U.S.C. § 20101 et seq. (Subtitle II). Congress declared in § 20102 that space activities shall be devoted to peaceful purposes for the benefit of all humankind, and that adequate provision for aeronautical and space activities is necessary for the general welfare and security of the United States. These activities are the responsibility of, and shall be directed by, a civilian agency. Section 20111 establishes NASA as an independent agency headed by a presidentially appointed, Senate-confirmed Administrator who exercises all powers and discharges all duties of the Administration under presidential supervision.

Section 20112 vests NASA with core operational functions: to plan, direct, and conduct aeronautical and space activities; to arrange scientific community participation in planning measurements and observations using aeronautical and space vehicles; and to disseminate information concerning its activities. Section 20113 grants NASA broad rulemaking authority to promulgate rules and regulations governing its operations and exercise of statutory powers. The Administrator may appoint up to 425 officers and employees without regard to civil service laws where deemed necessary.

Coordination with the Department of Defense occurs under § 20114, requiring mutual advice and consultation on matters within their respective jurisdictions. The Secretary of Defense retains authority to object if NASA's actions would adversely affect national security.

NASA's spaceflight safety regulations are promulgated under 14 CFR Part 1214, which establishes licensing and approval requirements for commercial space launch and reentry activities, reflecting the delegation of certain regulatory functions under the Commercial Space Launch Act while NASA retains direct operational authority over its own missions and programs under its organic statute.
