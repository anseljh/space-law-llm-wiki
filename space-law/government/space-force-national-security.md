# Space Force & National Security Space

*The United States Space Force and national-security space programs (10 U.S.C. ch. 908 and ch. 135).*

## Summary

The United States Space Force operates under delegated authority from the Secretary of Defense and the President to develop, acquire, and operate national security space capabilities. The governing statutory framework in 10 U.S.C. chapters 135 and 908 establishes organizational structures, acquisition standards, and strategic policies binding Defense Department space activities.

At the operational level, 10 U.S.C. § 2271 mandates that space development and acquisition programs be conducted through joint program offices with cross-service officer participation, ensuring integration across Army, Navy, Marine Corps, and Air Force. The Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Space Acquisition and Integration serves as the service acquisition executive and maintains a contractor responsibility watch list under § 2271a, establishing performance standards for space procurement. § 2272 requires the Secretary of Defense and Director of National Intelligence to jointly develop a biennial space science and technology strategy, creating coordination mechanisms between defense and intelligence communities.

Strategic policy is codified in § 2273, which establishes as national policy the President's obligation to ensure assured access to space for national security payloads, including maintenance of at least two independent space launch vehicle families. Within the Space Force, § 2273a establishes the Space Rapid Capabilities Office, reporting to the Chief of Space Operations but physically separated from Space Systems Command headquarters, enabling rapid acquisition pathways.

Defensive requirements are mandated by § 2275a: before major satellite programs achieve acquisition milestone approval, the Chief of Staff of the Space Force—consulting with United States Space Command—must establish defense and resilience requirements against adversary capabilities. § 2274 permits the Secretary of Defense to provide space situational awareness services to non-government entities under specified conditions, balancing operational sharing with security controls.

These provisions collectively establish integrated governance linking acquisition, strategy, defense requirements, and interagency coordination.
