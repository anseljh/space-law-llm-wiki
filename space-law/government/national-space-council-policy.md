# National Space Council & Space Policy

*Executive coordination of national space policy, including reviving the National Space Council (EO 13803).*

## Summary

The National Space Council operates under authority delegated to the President by the Constitution and statutory law, most recently reestablished by Executive Order 13803 (82 FR 31429, July 7, 2017). The Council's statutory foundation originates in the National Aeronautics and Space Act of 1958 and subsequent legislation establishing its charter, composition, and advisory role. EO 13803 explicitly reasserts presidential authority to establish a coordinated interagency mechanism for developing and monitoring implementation of national space policy and strategy.

The operative legal standard governing the Council's function is the President's general authority to coordinate executive branch agencies and establish policy-making bodies through executive order, subject to statutory constraints and appropriations law. The Council does not possess independent regulatory authority; rather, it functions as an advisory and coordinating body within the Executive Office of the President, similar to other presidential councils. Its authority derives from the President's constitutional power to "take Care that the Laws be faithfully executed" and extends to directing the implementation of space-related statutes administered by constituent agencies.

The structural arrangement interlocks the Council with agency heads responsible for space activities—principally the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), the Department of Defense, the Department of Commerce, and other relevant departments and agencies. The President, typically through the Vice President as Chair or a designated assistant, directs the Council's work. Constituent agencies retain their statutory authorities and responsibilities; the Council coordinates policy without displacing agency mandates. This design ensures that presidential space strategy drives interagency implementation while preserving agency statutory authority and accountability. The Council's operative scope encompasses civil, national security, and commercial space sectors, making it the primary civilian-side mechanism for comprehensive national space strategy formulation.
