# Space Resource Exploration & Utilization (SPACE Act 2015)

*Commercial rights to recover and use space resources under 51 U.S.C. ch. 513 and EO 13914 on international support for recovery and use of space resources.*

## Summary

The Commercial Space Launch Competitiveness Act of 2015 (codified at 51 U.S.C. chapter 513) establishes a federal legal framework granting United States citizens property rights in space resources extracted from celestial bodies. The statute defines "space resources" as abiotic resources in situ in outer space, expressly including water and minerals, with "asteroid resources" denoting those found on or within a single asteroid. The operative standard in 51 U.S.C. § 51302 directs federal policy to facilitate commercial exploration and recovery of space resources while discouraging government barriers to economically viable, safe, and stable domestic industries engaged in such activities—all "in manners consistent with the international obligations of the United States."

The substantive right is established in 51 U.S.C. § 51303: a United States citizen engaged in commercial recovery of an asteroid resource or space resource is "entitled to any asteroid resource or space resource obtained, including to possess, own, transport, use, and sell" the extracted resource "in accordance with applicable law, including the international obligations of the United States." This structure creates a conditional property entitlement: extraction and ownership rights attach upon successful commercial recovery, contingent on compliance with both domestic law and U.S. international treaty obligations.

Executive Order 13914 (April 10, 2020), citing title IV of the SPACE Act itself as its authority, further commits the United States to encouraging international support for space resource recovery and use. The executive order operationalizes statutory policy by directing federal agencies to pursue diplomatic recognition of extraction rights consistent with the Act's framework. Together, these authorities create a harmonized legal regime: domestic property rights in extracted resources paired with commitments to resolve international compatibility and establish reciprocal recognition among space-faring nations.
